Wildfire

Wildfires are uncontrolled fires that spread rapidly through vegetation, fueled by dry conditions, high temperatures, and strong winds. They can devastate forests, wildlife, and human settlements, causing significant environmental and economic damage. Preventive measures and prompt response are critical to reducing the risks and impacts of wildfires.

Hurricane

Hurricanes are powerful tropical storms characterized by strong winds, heavy rain, and storm surges, forming over warm ocean waters. They can cause widespread destruction to coastal areas, including flooding, wind damage, and power outages. Preparedness, early warnings, and evacuation plans are essential to minimize the impact on lives and property.

Earthquake

Earthquakes commonly occur along the edges of tectonic plates, where they interact whether through collision, sliding past each other, or pulling apart. These boundaries include transform, convergent, and divergent plate boundaries.Fault Lines: Earthquakes are often triggered by the movement along faults, which are fractures in the Earth's crust where blocks of rock have moved. Stress accumulation and sudden release along these faults result in seismic activity.

Flood

Floods are natural disasters caused by excessive rainfall, river overflow, or coastal storms, leading to water inundating normally dry land. They can result in significant damage to property, infrastructure, and agriculture, and pose serious risks to human life. Effective flood management and early warning systems are crucial to minimizing impact.
